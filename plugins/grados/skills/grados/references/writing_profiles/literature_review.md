# GRaDOS Literature Review Profile

Use this profile for literature reviews, related-work sections, state-of-the-art
surveys, theory background, and proposal background sections.

## Deliverable

Default output:

- scope and inclusion/exclusion notes;
- taxonomy or theme map;
- evidence grid or claim matrix summary;
- major methods, mechanisms, and results by theme;
- disagreements, limitations, and unresolved questions;
- concise synthesis paragraphs with inline citations;
- evidence status and missing full-text notes.

## Workflow

1. Convert the topic into English search queries with synonyms, mechanism terms,
   method terms, and domain-specific exclusions.
2. Search local and remote sources. Keep partially relevant papers when they
   reveal vocabulary, competing mechanisms, benchmark methods, or limitations.
3. Extract full text for papers that are likely to support central claims.
4. Build an evidence grid first when the field is broad; promote only durable
   factual statements into the claim matrix.
5. Draft from a section plan:
   `background`, `mechanisms`, `methods`, `applications`, `limitations`, and
   `research_gap` are common sections, but use the user's actual structure.
6. Audit theme-level and gap-level claims. Broad claims such as "most studies"
   or "recent work increasingly" require especially strong coverage evidence.

## Review Gates

Hard fail:

- citing papers that were not reread through canonical GRaDOS windows;
- making trend, novelty, consensus, or gap claims from a tiny or biased sample
  without saying so;
- using a paper's abstract as final evidence when full text was available;
- merging contradictory mechanisms into one smooth story without noting the
  disagreement.

Warning:

- search coverage is narrow in year range, publisher, discipline, language, or
  method family;
- review scope mixes simulation, theory, and experiment without clear labels;
- citation trail suggests a key older or review paper that has not been read.

## Output Rule

Prefer a compact taxonomy plus claim-backed synthesis over a paper-by-paper
annotated list. Use paper-by-paper notes only when the user asks for them or
when the field is too immature for a stable taxonomy.
